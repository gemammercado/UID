Nine Value Scale

What is value?

Value refers to how light or dark a color is. It helps define forms, create depth, and provide spatial relation. Lightness or darkness helps define the focal point in an artwork.

Rembrandt, The Storm on the Sea of Galilee

In Rembrandt's Storm on the Sea of Galilee, the lightest values, on the left, draw the viewer's eye towards the disciples at the front of the ship.

On the right, we see an example of the flat style of early Islamic art, where the figures are painted in solid colors, making the scene appear two-dimensional.

Shade vs. Tint

Shade is created when black is added to a color, making it darker. Think of stepping into the shade on a sunny day - colors appear darker.

Tint is created by adding white to a color, making it lighter.
