Lesson 3: Color Schemes

Complementary Colors

Complementary colors are opposite each other on the color wheel. They create high contrast when paired together.

Elina Jermaka, Oranges on Blue Background

This painting uses the complementary pair blue and orange, making both colors more vibrant than if they were standalone.

Secondary Colors

Secondary colors are those on the color wheel that are made directly from mixing the primary colors. Purple, for example, is made by mixing red and blue. Green is made by mixing yellow and blue.

Claude Monet, Water Lilies

Tertiary Colors

Tertiary colors are created by mixing a primary color with a secondary color on the color wheel. There are six tertiary colors: red-violet, blue-violet, blue-green, yellow-green, red-orange, and yellow-orange.

Louis Fratino, Cato

In Cato, Fratino mixes the secondary color green with blues and yellows to deepen or lighten the hue of the green. This is a great example of how tertiary colors can be used to create depth and dimension in a painting.
