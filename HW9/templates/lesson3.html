Complementary Colors

Complementary colors are pairs of colors which, when placed next to each other, they create the strongest contrast. They are opposite each other on the color wheel.

Elina Jermaka, Oranges on Blue Background

This painting uses the complementary pair blue and orange. Notice how the orange pops against the blue background, and the blue looks richer next to the oranges. Hover over the colored circles and see this for yourself.

Analogous Colors

Analogous colors are groups of colors that are adjacent to each other on the color wheel, such as red, orange, and red-orange. One color usually dominates, while others enrich the scheme.

Claude Monet, Water Lilies

Monet's Water Lilies series often employs analogous color schemes. This detail shows blues, greens, and violets, colors that sit next to each other on the wheel, creating harmony.

Triadic Colors

A triadic color scheme uses colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel. Triadic schemes tend to be quite vibrant, even if you use pale or unsaturated versions of your hues.
